Who is Ki Hadjar Dewantara?

Ki Hadjar Dewantoro, Dewantoro also spelled Dewantara, original name Raden Mas (Lord) Suwardi Surjaningrat, (born May 2, 1889, Yogyakarta, Java, Dutch East Indies [now Indonesia]—died April 26, 1959, Yogyakarta), founder of the Taman Siswa (literally “Garden of Students”) school system, an influential and widespread network of schools that encouraged modernization but also promoted indigenous Indonesian culture. Ki hadjar Dewantara was first Minister of National Education of the Republic of Indonesia. In addition, he was declared as the Father of Indonesian National Education and his birthday is decided as National Education Day.

Besides contributing in education, he was active in social and politic movements. He founded Boemipoetra Comitee who actively criticized Dutch East indies policy. He also involved in Boedi Oetomo organization activities such as promoting public awarness of Indonesia as national unity. He also organized Boedi Oetomo's first congress in Yogyakarta.
